About

I am currently a pre-doctoral research assistant at University College London (UCL), gratefully funded and supervised by Prof Caswell Barry . I work closely with people in UCL Cell and Developmental Biology, Centre for Artificial Intelligence, the Sainsbury Wellcome Centre and the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit.

In the past, I obtained my Bachelor of Science in Natural Sciences (major in Neuroscience, minor in Mathematics and Statistics), and subsequently remained a UCL alumni scholar for the Master of Science in Machine Learning, where I worked with Caswell for my masters' project.

Interests

Theoretical neuroscience studies the nervous system employing methods and theories from neurobiology, mathematics and physics, machine learning, information theory and signal processing, statistical mechanics, and many more. Below is a brief summary of my interests:

  • Probabilistic (or Bayesian) computations in the brain and neural activity analysis: receptive field models, population codes, neural representation of uncertainty, predictive coding, etc..
  • The associated approximate Bayesian inference schemes, mostly recognition models, inspired by or inspiring the above mechanisms.
  • Machine learning applied to neural data modelling: supervised (deep) learning of neural circuits, dimensionality reduction (PCA, GPFA, etc.).
  • Spatial learning, navigation and relational memory: how place cells, grid cells, etc. work together for much of the EC/HPC functions.

Teaching

I am teaching assistant for COMP0085 Approximate Inference and Learning in Probabilistic Models (2022-23), run jointly by the Gatsby Unit and CS and led by Maneesh Sahani. This module introduces a wide range of approximate inference schemes in latent variable models, as well as some exact inference and supervised learning methods.
