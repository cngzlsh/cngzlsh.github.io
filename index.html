Welcome to Sihao Liu (Daniel) 's GitHub page.

I am currently a postgraduate student at University College London (UCL).

Staring this September I will be a pre-doctoral research assistant working on theoretical (or computational) neuroscience, under the supervision of Caswell Barry, supported by Turing AI Fellowship. I work closely with people in UCL Cell and Developmental Biology, Centre for Artificial Intelligence, the Sainsbury Wellcome Centre and the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit.

Theoretical neuroscience studies the nervous system emplying methods and theories from neurobiology, mathematics,, machine learning, information theory and signal processing, statistical physics, and many more. Below is a brief summary of my interests:

  • Probabilistic (or Bayesian) computations in the brain: receptive field models, population codes, neural representation of uncertainty, predictive coding, etc..
  • The associated approximate Bayesian inference schemes, mostly recognition models, inspired by or inspiring the above mechanisms.
  • Spatial learning, navigation and relational memory: how place cells, grid cells, etc. work together for much of the EC/HPC functions.

In the past, I obtained my Bachelor of Science in Natural Sciences (major in Neuroscience, minor in Mathematics and Statistics) at UCL, and subsequently remained a UCL alumni scholar for the Master of Science in Machine Learning, to be completed in September.
